I've changed the internet

February 7, 2026

I own a copy of Borges’ Atlas. It is, I hope, waiting in a box with many other books, in my childhood house, on the other side of the Atlantic.

I remember looking at the photographs in a bookstore on Av. Corrientes and reading the whole book on my way home.

One text struck me. I have not committed it to memory, and even if I had it before my eyes my poor English skills would not do justice to his style. But the idea in that fragment is so powerful that my dubious prose can do little to reduce its impact.

As I remember it:

He walked over the sand, knelt and grabbed a fistful of it. Then, he let it fall between his fingers, and said “I’ve changed the desert.”

Like him, I’m here in front of the vast Internet, unable to see more than glimpses of it.

May the post in this blog be my grains of sand.
